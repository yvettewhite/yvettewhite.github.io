THE PROCESS

The Beginning Stage - The Plan

We started with nothing but a sketch and a shared vision. As a family, we planted, planned, and nurtured this yard together. Years later, it’s still a work in progress, but it’s slowly becoming the space we dreamed of.

We dreamed of building the ultimate embankment slide right in our yard. Our first attempt? Repurposing an old Jimmy John’s billboard tarp as a barrier to stop the hillside from eroding—definitely creative, if a little unconventional! Buying a commercial embankment slide was next on the list, but the price made us think twice. Inspired by the harvest festival slides we’d seen each fall, we decided to take matters into our own hands.

We special-ordered a 5-foot-wide, 20-foot-long irrigation pipe, cut it in half, and connected the pieces to create a 40-foot adventure right at home. Now, whether it’s toddlers enjoying a gentle ride or adults feeling some serious butterflies on a sled—or even a modified snoboard (boot clamps removed) this slide has become a thrill zone for all ages.

Building the Trampoline

The trampoline area was constructed with both safety and fun in mind. Reinforced frames and soft landings ensure hours of enjoyment for kids and adults alike.

Designing the Waterfall

Using natural stones and recycled water flow, the backyard waterfall creates a serene atmosphere perfect for relaxation and birdwatching.

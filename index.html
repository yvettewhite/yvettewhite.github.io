Welcome to Our Yard

Back in 2016, we stood at the edge of a dusty backyard with a big dream and a rough sketch on paper. We mapped out everything we wanted: a spot for the trampoline, a cozy firepit, a peaceful waterfall, and a court for games and gatherings.

When the bids came in, we had a serious case of sticker shock. So instead of shelving the dream, we sold our travel trailer and bought a mini-excavator. From that day on, it became a true family project. We sorted and hauled rocks by hand, carved trenches through stubborn clay, laid pipes, pulled electrical wires, planted so many plants, and positioned every piece of the rock walls and stone steps ourselves, sometimes twice (okay, maybe three times) to get it right.

It’s still a work in progress, but now it’s more than just a yard, it’s our story, built one paver at a time. Today, we’re honored to share this space with others. Over the years, our yard has hosted wedding receptions, a wedding proposal, backyard concerts, birthday parties, and so many BBQ and smores nights. Every glowing fire, every light strung overhead, and every yard game under the trees reminds us the hard work was worth it.

Flowers in my Yard

If my backyard had a social director, it would be the salvia. These bold, spiky blooms are always buzzing—literally—with bees, butterflies, and the occasional hummingbird. Their vivid purples and deep blues add instant drama, and they keep blooming long after other flowers call it quits. Salvia is basically the extrovert and the workhorse of the garden.

Not exactly a flower, but too enchanting to leave out my Marguerite potato vines bring that luminous chartreuse pop. They grow fast, fill space, and tie all the colors in the yard together.

Decidious Trees in the Yard

If you’ve ever stood under the shade of a big tree on a hot summer day, you already know: trees don’t just grow—they give. Adding even one or two trees to your yard can completely transform your home, your space, and even your daily mood. They’re the quiet, dependable anchor to our yard.

There’s something about looking at leaves dancing in the wind that just melts stress away. Studies show that being around trees lowers anxiety, improves focus, and boosts our overall wellbeing. A tree in the yard isn’t just landscaping, it’s therapy. Each tree species was chosen for its unique characteristics and seasonal changes, creating a diverse and engaging landscape year-round.
